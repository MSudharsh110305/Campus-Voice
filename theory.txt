1. PROBLEM STATEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Engineering college students face numerous day-to-day grievances — broken
infrastructure, mess food quality, hostel maintenance, academic issues,
disciplinary concerns — yet have no structured, transparent channel to raise
and track them. The existing approach relies on informal verbal complaints,
physical complaint registers, or direct emails, resulting in:

  • Complaints getting lost or ignored with no tracking mechanism.
  • Students not knowing who to contact for which issue.
  • Authorities unaware of urgency or the scale of a problem.
  • No accountability — no audit trail of what was done and when.
  • Repeated complaints about the same issue going unnoticed.
  • Misrouting — hostel issues going to the wrong warden, academic
    issues going to admin instead of the HOD.
  • Students afraid to complain due to lack of anonymity.
  • No way for peers to collectively signal that an issue is widespread.

Campus Voice solves all of these by providing a centralized, AI-driven,
transparent, and role-based digital grievance management platform.

2. OBJECTIVES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  1. Provide a single digital platform for students to raise campus complaints
     anonymously, attach photo evidence, and track resolution status.

  2. Use Artificial Intelligence (LLM) to automatically:
       a. Classify complaints into the correct category.
       b. Identify the target department.
       c. Detect spam, abuse, and gibberish automatically.
       d. Rephrase informal language into a formal complaint.
       e. Verify that uploaded images are relevant to the complaint.
       f. Determine if a complaint type requires photographic evidence.

  3. Implement a smart authority routing system that sends each complaint
     directly to the right person (Warden / HOD / Admin Officer / Disciplinary
     Committee) based on complaint content — no manual categorization needed.

  4. Build a democratic priority system where student upvotes/downvotes raise
     or lower the urgency of a complaint, ensuring widespread issues are
     addressed first.

  5. Enforce automatic escalation — complaints unresolved for more than 7 days
     are escalated up the authority hierarchy without manual intervention.

  6. Give students real-time notifications and full visibility into their
     complaint lifecycle.

  7. Provide authorities with dashboards, analytics, and tools to manage their
     workload efficiently.

  8. Give the Admin complete control: manage users, authorities, bulk operations,
     image moderation, and system-wide analytics.

  9. Empower student leaders (Representatives) to raise Petitions for systemic or
     large-scale changes, gathering signatures to meet custom milestones and
     prompt official authority responses.

  10. Highlight success stories through a "Wins" (Changelog) feed, showcasing
      resolved popular issues to build trust in the platform.

7. AI / LLM FEATURES (GROQ API — LLaMA)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All AI processing is done through the Groq API (fast LLM inference).
Primary model: meta-llama/llama-3.1-8b-instant (text)
Vision model: meta-llama/llama-4-scout-17b-16e-instruct (image)

Every LLM call is logged to LLMProcessingLog with full prompt, response,
token count, processing time, and cost for auditability.

Retry strategy: Exponential backoff (min 2s, max 30s, max 3 retries).
Fallback: If LLM unavailable, keyword-based classification is used.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1 COMPLAINT CATEGORIZATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What it does:
  Automatically assigns the correct category and department to a complaint
  based purely on what the text says — no manual selection by the student.

Output JSON:
  {
    "category": "Men's Hostel" | "Women's Hostel" | "General" |
                "Department" | "Disciplinary Committee",
    "target_department": "CSE" | "ECE" | "MECH" | ... (17 options),
    "priority": "Low" | "Medium" | "High" | "Critical",
    "reasoning": "Max 40-word explanation",
    "confidence": 0.0–1.0,
    "is_against_authority": true|false,
    "requires_image": true|false
  }

Categorization Rules (in order of precedence):
  1. Behavioral misconduct (ragging, violence, cheating, disruption)
     → "Disciplinary Committee"
  2. Academic/department facilities (labs, projectors, classrooms, faculty)
     → "Department" + matching department code
  3. Hostel facilities (rooms, mess, bathrooms, water, electricity)
     → Gender-specific hostel based on complaint text or student gender
  4. Campus-wide (roads, parking, wifi, canteen, library, auditorium)
     → "General"

Academic Override (post-processing):
  If LLM returns hostel category BUT text contains academic keywords
  (lab, classroom, faculty, professor, exam, software, seminar hall, etc.),
  the category is overridden to "Department" deterministically.

Department Detection (17 departments):
  CSE→computer science lab / ECE→electronics / MECH→mechanical workshop /
  CIVIL→surveying / EEE→electrical / IT→IT lab / AIDS→AI, data science /
  MTECH_CSE→mtech / MBA→management / ENG/PHY/CHEM/MATH→subject names

Priority Assignment:
  Critical: Safety hazards, fire, violence, medical emergency
  High:     Major infrastructure failures, large impact, no water/power
  Medium:   Moderate inconveniences, partial failures
  Low:      Minor issues, cosmetic problems

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2 COMPLAINT REPHRASING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What it does:
  Converts the student's informal, possibly grammatically incorrect, or
  emotionally charged text into a formal, clear, 1-2 sentence complaint.

Rules enforced by prompt:
  • Maximum 50 words, 1–2 sentences
  • Fix grammar and spelling
  • Remove offensive/emotional language
  • Preserve the core issue and key details (location, time, severity)
  • Do NOT invent information not present in original

Special case — Gibberish detection:
  If the input is random characters, keyboard mashing (e.g. "asdfghjkl"),
  or meaningless sequences, the LLM returns the sentinel string "GIBBERISH".
  The system then treats the complaint as spam and rejects it.

Output: Used as rephrased_text in the complaint (shown publicly).
        Original text (original_text) is preserved for reference.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3 SPAM & ABUSE DETECTION (Two-Layer)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Layer 1 — Keyword Check (fast, synchronous):
  Scans complaint text against a SPAM_KEYWORDS list:
  ["spam", "test", "testing", "dummy", "fake", "xyz", "abc", "asdf",
   "qwerty", "junk", "trash", "nonsense", "gibberish", "123"]
  If matched → immediate spam flag without LLM call.

Layer 2 — LLM Spam Classification (async):
  Prompt asks LLM to determine if the complaint is spam.
  Output: { "is_spam": true|false, "confidence": 0–1, "reason": "..." }

IS Spam if:
  • Random characters / keyboard mashing
  • No coherent meaning (gibberish)
  • Abusive or profane language
  • Joke / prank submission
  • Personal attack (not a campus issue)
  • Test / dummy content
  • Advertisement or promotion
  • Completely irrelevant content

NOT Spam (valid despite imperfections):
  • Spelling errors (real but poorly written)
  • Informal language with frustration ("this is so bad!!")
  • Short but genuine ("AC broken in lab 3")
  • Tamil/English code-switching (describes a real issue)
  • Mentions of authorities in complaint context

Outcome:
  • Flagged complaints are saved with is_marked_as_spam=true
  • Student + authority both notified
  • Authority can manually review and override
  • Admin can permanently blacklist repeat offenders

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.4 IMAGE REQUIREMENT DETECTION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What it does:
  Analyzes complaint text to determine if photographic evidence is needed.

REQUIRES IMAGE:
  Physical damage (broken furniture, cracks, burst pipes, electrical hazards,
  broken doors, mould, water leaking, stained walls, structural issues)

DOES NOT REQUIRE:
  Pest sightings (can't photograph on demand), staff absence, schedule
  violations, service failures, academic issues, harassment, waiting complaints

Default: false — a complaint is never blocked purely due to missing image
unless the LLM explicitly flags it as requiring one.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.5 IMAGE VERIFICATION (Vision API — LLaMA 4 Scout)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What it does:
  When a student uploads an image, the vision LLM checks whether it is:
  (a) relevant to the complaint text, and (b) not abusive/inappropriate.

Input: Base64 data URI of the image + complaint text
Output JSON:
  {
    "is_relevant": true|false,
    "confidence": 0.0–1.0,
    "reason": "Max 100-word explanation",
    "detected_objects": ["list", "of", "objects"],
    "visible_issues": ["visible", "problems"],
    "quality_rating": "Good" | "Fair" | "Poor",
    "is_appropriate": true|false
  }

Acceptance logic (lenient policy):
  ACCEPT if: Related to the complaint topic at all (food photo for food
  complaint, room photo for room complaint), genuine photograph even if
  issue is not perfectly visible, loosely related context photo.

  REJECT only if: Completely unrelated (selfie for plumbing complaint),
  meme/internet image, offensive/inappropriate content, obvious test image.

Markdown stripping:
  LLMs sometimes wrap JSON in ```json...``` code blocks. The system strips
  these before parsing so the response always parses correctly.

Status tracking:
  Pending → (admin moderates) → Verified | Rejected | Error

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.6 LLM FALLBACK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If the Groq API is unavailable:
  • Categorization falls back to keyword matching against category keyword lists
  • Spam detection falls back to keyword-only check
  • Rephrasing is skipped; original text is used as-is
  • Image verification falls back to "Pending" status (requires manual review)
  • Complaint submission still succeeds — no complaint is lost due to AI failure

8. COMPLAINT LIFECYCLE (End-to-End)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1: SUBMISSION
  Student fills in:
    - Complaint description (What happened? Where? When?) — 10–2000 chars
    - Visibility: Public | Private
    - Optional: Photo evidence (JPEG/PNG/WebP, max 5MB)

Step 2: SPAM CHECK (Layer 1 — Keyword)
  Instant check against spam keyword list.
  If matched → Reject immediately with HTTP 400.

Step 3: LLM SPAM CHECK (Layer 2 — AI)
  Groq LLM analyzes the text.
  If spam detected → complaint saved as Spam, student notified.

Step 4: LLM CATEGORIZATION
  LLM determines:
    • Category (which department/type)
    • Target department (which HOD/authority)
    • Priority (Low / Medium / High / Critical)
    • Whether an image is required
    • Whether it's against an authority (special routing)
    • Whether it's cross-department

Step 5: GIBBERISH / REPHRASING
  LLM rephrases text into a formal 1-2 sentence complaint.
  If gibberish detected → complaint rejected.

Step 6: IMAGE PROCESSING (if image uploaded)
  Pillow re-encodes image to JPEG (quality 85).
  200×200 thumbnail generated.
  Both stored as binary in PostgreSQL.
  LLM vision API checks relevance → sets image_verification_status.

Step 7: AUTHORITY ROUTING
  System finds the right authority based on category + department:
    Men's Hostel complaint  → Men's Hostel Warden
    Women's Hostel complaint → Women's Hostel Warden
    Department complaint    → HOD of that department
    General complaint       → Admin Officer
    Disciplinary complaint  → Disciplinary Committee
    Complaint against authority → Skip to higher level (Deputy Warden etc.)

Step 8: NOTIFICATION
  Assigned authority receives a notification: "New complaint assigned to you."

Step 9: STATUS = "Raised"
  Complaint is live in the system. Student can see it in "My Posts".
  If Public visibility, appears in Campus Feed for other students to vote on.

Step 10: VOTING (Community)
  Other students can upvote (supports the issue) or downvote (marks irrelevant).
  Vote score recalculates priority_score continuously.
  High vote scores can auto-escalate priority from Medium → High → Critical.

Step 11: AUTHORITY ACTION
  Authority reviews complaint and:
    • Updates status: Raised → In Progress
    • Posts public updates (progress messages)
    • Escalates if needed (to Deputy Warden / Admin Officer)
    • Resolves: In Progress → Resolved
    • Or closes without resolution: → Closed

Step 12: AUTO-ESCALATION (if no action for 7 days)
  System automatically escalates to next authority in hierarchy.
  original_assigned_authority_id saved for tracking.

Step 13: RESOLUTION
  Status → Resolved or Closed.
  Student notified.
  resolved_at timestamp recorded.

  STATUS FLOW:
  Raised → In Progress → Resolved → Closed
       ↘                         ↗
         Spam (at any active stage)

10. VOTING SYSTEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PURPOSE:
  Voting lets the student community signal which issues are widespread and
  urgent. High votes force the system to escalate priority — ensuring popular
  complaints are never buried.

MECHANICS:
  • POST /complaints/{id}/vote — body: { "vote_type": "Upvote" | "Downvote" }
  • DELETE /complaints/{id}/vote — remove vote
  • GET /complaints/{id}/my-vote — check your current vote
  • One vote per student per complaint (unique constraint in DB)
  • Clicking same vote type again removes it (toggle)
  • Clicking a different type replaces the current vote

SCORE FORMULA:
  vote_score        = (upvotes × 5) + (downvotes × -3)
  priority_score    = base_priority_score + (vote_score × 2.0)

  Base scores:
    Low      → 10.0
    Medium   → 50.0
    High     → 100.0
    Critical → 200.0

AUTO-PRIORITY ESCALATION:
  If priority_score reaches ≥ 150 (PRIORITY_AUTO_ESCALATE_THRESHOLD),
  the complaint's priority is automatically upgraded:
    score ≥ 200 → Critical
    score ≥ 100 → High
    score ≥ 50  → Medium
    score < 50  → Low

EXAMPLE:
  A "Medium" complaint (base 50) with 20 upvotes and 2 downvotes:
    vote_score = (20×5) + (2×−3) = 100 − 6 = 94
    priority_score = 50 + (94 × 2) = 238 → Critical priority

OWN VOTE PROTECTION:
  Students cannot vote on their own complaints.
  Backend returns HTTP 403. Frontend shows a friendly toast popup.

15. IMAGE STORAGE & HANDLING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DESIGN DECISION: All images stored as binary (BYTEA) directly in PostgreSQL.
No file system, no S3, no external storage. Fully self-contained.

UPLOAD FLOW:
  1. Student uploads file (≤5MB, JPEG/PNG/GIF/WebP).
  2. Pillow library re-encodes to JPEG (quality 85) for consistency.
  3. 200×200 thumbnail generated and stored separately.
  4. Both stored as binary columns in the Complaints table.
  5. LLM Vision API called to verify relevance.
  6. Admin can later moderate via /admin/images/{id}/moderate.

RETRIEVAL:
  GET /complaints/{id}/image           → Streams original binary with MIME type
  GET /complaints/{id}/image?thumbnail=true → Streams thumbnail

LIMITS:
  Max image size  : 5MB
  Max dimensions  : 4096×4096
  JPEG quality    : 85
  Allowed types   : JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP
  Per complaint   : Up to 2 images

VERIFICATION STATUSES:
  Pending  → Just uploaded, awaiting review
  Verified → LLM + admin approved
  Rejected → Irrelevant, offensive, or not matching complaint
  Error    → Processing failed (stored anyway for manual review)

DETECTION (Two layers — Section 7.3)

BLACKLISTING:

  • is_marked_as_spam boolean in complaint
  • spam_reason, spam_flagged_by, spam_flagged_at tracked
  • SpamBlacklist table for persistent offenders

22. ADVANCED BACKEND ALGORITHMS AND PIPELINES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

22.1 HYBRID DUPLICATE-DETECTION (SEMANTIC + FUZZY)
  • Purpose: Detect near-duplicate complaints before submission and auto-cluster repeated reports for merging.
  • Method: Five-signal hybrid similarity with cluster-weight gating:
    - Word Jaccard (weight 0.30): On normalized tokens.
    - Topic Jaccard (weight 0.20): On topic-only tokens (location words removed).
    - Bigram Jaccard (weight 0.10): On token-pairs.
    - Character n-gram Jaccard (weight 0.15): Trigrams on condensed text.
    - Fuzzy token match (weight 0.25): Using Levenshtein with length-dependent thresholds.
    - Cluster gate: Multiplies raw score by 1.3 for same-topic clusters or 0.30 for disjoint clusters.
  • Thresholds & Policy:
    - Candidate surfacing threshold = 0.12; likely-duplicate warning ≥ 0.25.
    - Auto-merge cluster if ≥ 10 members with per-member similarity ≥ 0.22.
    - Auto-merge uses an LLM to synthesize a canonical summary from up to 15 items; votes are deduplicated (keep most recent vote per student).
  • Implementation Notes: Synonym/normalization table, stop-word removal, topic clusters, trigram extraction, Levenshtein optimizations. (See code for exact token/synonym lists).

22.2 DETERMINISTIC CATEGORY OVERRIDES & SHORTFORM NORMALIZATION
  • Purpose: Correct systematic LLM misclassifications, preserve safety and policy constraints.
  • Techniques:
    - Pre-LLM Alias/Shortform Normalization: Comprehensive campus alias table; special handling for ambiguous shortforms like `ac`.
    - Post-LLM Deterministic Overrides: Academic override (promote hostel→Department when lab/academic tokens present), repair/facility override (Department→General for shared repairs), Disciplinary Committee rules (student-on-student only), and hostel→General overrides when non-hostel location tokens appear.
    - Unethical Check: Regexes pre-block grade manipulation/bribe-like requests.
  • Rationale: Deterministic, transparent rules to guard LLM routing.

22.3 HYBRID PRIORITY SCORING (MULTI-SIGNAL DETERMINISTIC + LLM)
  • Purpose: Produce a traceable initial priority from complaint text (anchor for later vote blending).
  • Signals (Deterministic; max totals shown):
    - Category baseline (max 30).
    - Urgency / safety keywords (max 25).
    - Scope / affected population (max 20).
    - Recurrence / duration (max 15).
  • Mapping (Score → Label):
    - Score ≥ 50 → Critical
    - 35 ≤ Score < 50 → High
    - 20 ≤ Score < 35 → Medium
    - Score < 20 → Low
  • Optional LLM Contextual Adjustment: LLM may suggest {-10, -5, 0, +5, +10} to fine-tune the deterministic score; final priority computed on adjusted score.
  • Implementation: Deterministic scorer with regex and keyword groups, plus async LLM adjustment call when configured.

22.4 VOTE-DRIVEN BLENDED PRIORITY (WILSON + ENGAGEMENT)
  • Purpose: Let community voting influence priority without letting votes fully override anchor assessment.
  • Core Techniques:
    - Wilson score lower bound for upvote ratio (95% CI) to get a conservative estimate of the true upvote proportion.
    - Engagement factor (based on votes / reach) multiplies Wilson score to reward high participation.
    - Blended priority = initial anchor base score + vote contribution + engagement bonus. Anchor base map: Low=12, Medium=27, High=42, Critical=60.
  • Equations (as implemented):
    - Wilson lower bound w for upvotes u, downvotes d:
      w = (p + (z^2)/(2n) - z*sqrt((p(1-p)+(z^2)/(4n))/n)) / (1+(z^2)/n),  p=u/n, n=u+d
      (z=1.96 for 95% CI)
    - vote_rate = (u+d) / max(reach, 1)
    - engagement_factor = 1 + 2 * vote_rate
    - adjusted_score ≈ w * 100 * engagement_factor
    - Cap on vote contribution (±30 points), engagement bonus up to +10 points. Final blended score clamped to [0,100].
  • Safety Guard: Priority can change by at most one level per vote update.
  • Vote Filtering: Only votes from students eligible to view the complaint are counted (e.g., men's-hostel votes only from male hostel students).

22.5 SPAM DETECTION & BLACKLISTING
  • Purpose: Detect and mitigate low-value / malicious submissions.
  • Techniques:
    - Pre-LLM heuristics: Short-length rejection, regex detection of "grade manipulation" requests.
    - Keyword list with Levenshtein-based fuzzy matching: Exact substring for short/multi-word spam keys. Fuzzy match for single-word keywords against each word in text (threshold: 1 for 4-5 letter keys, 2 for ≥6 letters).
    - Persistent spam blacklist in DB with temporary/permanent bans and expiry logic.
  • Behavior: Flagged as spam (Spam status) or student blacklisted per policy.

22.6 LLM COMBINED PROCESSING PIPELINE
  • Purpose: Single LLM call to produce spam check, category, target department, rephrasing, image requirement, and reasoning (improves latency & token usage).
  • Design:
    - Pre-LLM regex guard against off-topic/prompt-injection.
    - Combined prompt returns a single JSON structure.
    - Deterministic keyword fallback used if LLM is unavailable.
  • Implementation Notes: Structured prompts, JSON extraction robust to code fences, deterministic post-processing overrides applied to LLM output.

22.7 IMAGE VERIFICATION (VISION LLM + FALLBACK)
  • Purpose: Automatically verify whether uploaded image evidence is relevant to the complaint.
  • Method:
    - Images stored as binary and encoded to data URI for Vision LLM calls.
    - Vision LLM prompt is lenient (accept-by-default) returning JSON.
    - Thresholds: Strict rejection only for confidence < ~0.3-0.4 with clear mismatch. Wait for manual check default if fallback.
  • Context: Verified images strengthen complaints, irrelevant images can convert to Spam.

22.8 AUTHORITY ROUTING AND BYPASS RULES
  • Purpose: Map categorized complaints to the correct authority and handle complaints about authority figures.
  • Rules:
    - Default routing: category → authority type.
    - Bypass rules: Complaints ABOUT an authority are assigned to the next-level authority to avoid conflict of interest.
    - Minimum authority levels used to select an authority at or above a level.

22.9 IMAGE-GRACE WORKFLOW, REACH, AND VISIBILITY
  • Image Grace: When LLM marks an image as required but none provided, complaint is posted with `image_pending` flag and a 24-hour upload deadline. Authority notified if not uploaded.
  • Reach Calculation: Number of eligible viewers computed from DB used in vote damping and engagement formulas.
  • Visibility Rules: Disciplinary Committee complaints forced Private.

22.10 IMPLEMENTATION POINTERS & EXACT PARAMETERS
  • Duplicate Candidate Surfacing: 0.12 threshold (warn ≥ 0.25).
  • Auto-merge: min cluster size = 10, per-member similarity ≥ 0.22, LLM cap 15 items.
  • Priority Weights: Deterministic maxima (30, 25, 20, 15). Final thresholds: Critical≥50, High≥35, Medium≥20.
  • Vote Damping: Wilson z = 1.96. Vote cap ±30 + engagement bonus (up to +10).
  • Image Verification: Conf < ~0.3 (strict reject).
  • Fuzzy Spam: Levenshtein limit relies strictly on key length.

23. FEED SORTING ALGORITHMS (HOT, NEW, TOP)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

To provide a dynamic and engaging campus feed, we implement algorithms for 'Hot', 'New', and 'Top' sorting, allowing users to discover both recent and important grievances efficiently.

23.1 NEW (Chronological)
  • Formula: Sort completely based on `submitted_at` in descending order.
  • Purpose: Provides a raw feed of the most recently submitted issues.

23.2 TOP (Net Votes / Pure Popularity)
  • Formula: Sort by overall engagement score or purely by (`upvotes` - `downvotes`) descending.
  • Purpose: Shows the most universally agreed-upon issues of all time (or within a specific timeframe like "Top this month"). Highly effective for uncovering systemic, long-lasting campus grievances.

23.3 HOT (Reddit-style Hotness Algorithm)
  • Formula: Based on the Hacker News or Reddit 'Hot' algorithm, balancing upvotes against time decay.
  • Example Implementation:
    Score = log10(max(|u - d|, 1)) + (Sign(u - d) * (t_submitted - t_epoch) / 45000)
    where t_epoch is a predefined past date (e.g., project inception), and 45000 is a tuning parameter matching gravity or time-decay factor (e.g., higher values = slower decay).
  • Purpose: Surfaces rapidly gaining complaints (high engagement in a short time) while allowing older highly upvoted complaints to naturally fall off the feed.

26. USER POV: HOW THE FLOW WORKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

From a student's perspective, Campus Voice isn't just a form to fill out — it's
a lively, democratic community platform where issues are surfaced, supported, and
visibly resolved.

1. DISCOVERY & AWARENESS (The Campus Feed)
   When a student logs in, they aren't greeted by an empty dashboard, but by the
   "Campus Feed". Here, they see the current heartbeat of campus life: complaints
   raised by their peers about broken lab PCs, hostel water shortages, or
   general campus infrastructure. They can scroll through these issues, seeing
   what's trending, how severe each is, and which authority is handling them.

2. RAISING A VOICE (Smart Submission)
   When a student encounters a problem (e.g., "The fan in classroom 304 is making
   loud noises"), they don't need to write a formal letter or figure out if
   it goes to the HOD, the Admin Officer, or Maintenance. They just type what
   they experienced in plain, informal language, snap a photo if needed, and hit
   submit. Behind the scenes, the AI understands the context, corrects the grammar,
   labels it "Department", and directs it straight to the relevant HOD. The student
   is immediately presented with a clean, formal complaint card.

3. BUILDING MOMENTUM (Voting & Petitions)
   If an issue affects multiple people, the student doesn't suffer alone. Their
   complaint appears in the public feed, where peers can upvote it. As votes pile
   up, the background system automatically turns up the heat — moving the issue
   from "Medium" to "High" to "Critical" priority. 
   
   For massive, policy-level changes (like extending library hours), a Student
   Representative can bypass normal complaints and launch a formal "Petition". Once
   approved, the whole student body signs it. Hitting milestones automatically rings
   the alarm for higher authorities, showing undisputed collective demand.

4. TRANSPARENCY & COMMUNICATION (Notifications)
   Students are never left in the dark. If an authority starts working on their
   complaint, their phone chimes with a notification. If an authority posts a
   custom response on a petition or drops an announcement affecting their specific
   hostel bloc, the student sees it immediately.

5. CELEBRATING SUCCESS (The Wins Feed)
   When the fan is finally fixed, the HOD marks the complaint as "Resolved".
   The student gets the good news and leaves a satisfaction rating. The complaint
   is then proudly displayed in the "Wins" tab alongside the authority's resolution
   note. Instead of focusing only on broken things, students browse the Wins feed
   and see a proven track record of the college listening and taking action.

27. AUTHORITY POV: MANAGING THE CAMPUS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

From an authority's perspective (like a Warden, HOD, or Admin Officer), Campus
Voice is a tool that brings order to chaos, prioritizing what truly matters and
cutting out manual triage.

1. NO MORE MISROUTED COMPLAINTS
   Instead of receiving complaints meant for another department, the system’s AI
   ensures authorities only see what they are supposed to fix. A Men's Hostel
   Warden sees issues restricted to their hostel, while an HOD only sees
   academic or departmental infrastructure issues.

2. CUTTING THROUGH NOISE
   Authorities don't need to read through hundreds of identical complaints. Spam
   and gibberish are instantly blocked by the AI. When a legitimate issue is
   submitted, the authority sees a single, clean dashboard with an AI-rephrased,
   easy-to-read summary rather than a block of emotional text. 

3. DEMOCRATIC PRIORITIZATION
   Rather than guessing which broken window to fix first, the authority looks at
   the priority badges. If an issue is upgraded to "Critical" because hundreds of
   students upvoted it or signed a Petition, the authority immediately knows it
   requires urgent attention. 

4. DIRECT & SCALABLE COMMUNICATION
   Instead of printing notices on public boards, authorities can shoot targeted
   announcements directly to specific student groups—like "All female hostel
   students" or "All first-year CSE day scholars." If a major issue is being
   fixed, they can post a status update on the complaint, instantly notifying
   everyone who cared about it.

5. ACCOUNTABILITY & ESCALATION
   Authorities know that if they let a complaint sit for over 7 days, it will
   auto-escalate to their superior. This creates a transparent chain of
   accountability, ensuring that issues don't fall through the cracks and are
   resolved in a timely manner.

28. ADMIN POV: GOVERNING THE PLATFORM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

From an Administrator's perspective (the absolute highest level of the system), Campus
Voice is a comprehensive oversight and governance tool. The Admin ensures the entire 
ecosystem is functioning fairly, smoothly, and securely.

1. BIRD'S-EYE SYSTEM VISIBILITY
   While Wardens and HODs see only their segmented piece of the pie, the Admin sees
   everything. The global analytics dashboard highlights bottlenecks — for example, if a
   particular department is consistently missing its 7-day SLA or if a specific hostel
   is experiencing a sudden surge in infrastructure complaints.

2. MANAGING THE AUTHORITIES
   The Admin serves as the gatekeeper. They onboard new authorities, reset passwords,
   and delegate system roles. If a Warden is on leave, the Admin can quickly adjust
   the routing or assign a substitute to prevent complaints from stalling.

3. ESCALATION & CRISIS MANAGEMENT
   When severe issues go ignored for more than 7 days, they land directly on the Admin's
   escalation dashboard. The Admin can trace the full audit log to see exactly where
   the delay occurred and hold the original authority accountable, stepping in to close
   the gap on critical priorities.

4. PLATFORM INTEGRITY & MODERATION
   The Admin has the ultimate say on spam, abuse, and platform health. They review the
   system’s automated "Pending Verification" images to maintain community standards.
   If a student persistently abuses the platform, the Admin can apply temporary or
   permanent blacklists, preserving the system's reputation and ensuring authorities
   are only spending time on legitimate grievances.

5. DATA-DRIVEN POLICY MAKING
   By analyzing long-term trends from the system health and time-series analytics, 
   the Admin can make overarching policy and budget decisions (e.g., "We had 400 
   complaints about Wi-Fi in the library this semester; it's time for an infrastructure upgrade").

Campus Voice is a comprehensive, production-grade campus grievance management
system that leverages modern AI capabilities to remove friction from both ends:

  • Students no longer need to know who to contact — AI routes it correctly.
  • Students no longer need to worry about formal language — AI rephrases it.
  • Authorities no longer face a backlog of irrelevant complaints — AI filters spam.
  • Widespread issues surface automatically via the voting + priority system.
  • No complaint is ever permanently buried — auto-escalation ensures accountability.
  • The full audit trail means every decision is traceable and transparent.

By combining a robust FastAPI backend, PostgreSQL data model, React frontend,
and Groq LLM integration, Campus Voice delivers a scalable, intelligent, and
student-friendly grievance platform ready for real-world deployment at SREC.
